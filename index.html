The Shusaku Study Project

The key information

What:
Analyze Shusaku games every week
Why:
To provide public-domain commentary of Shusaku games
When:
May 2022 -> ? (Estimated time to finish: 2033)
Who:
Anyone who can dedicate a few hours a week to this. Preferably at least SDK, but if you're dedicated, you'll grow with us!
How:
A three-pass analysis of each Shusaku game, using katago as a basis, with pro-level amateurs providing guidance
Where:
Trevoke's Discord server

Intro

Hi all, I'm looking for folks who are interested in dedicating some time out of their week, for the next few years, to analyzing Shusaku games. The more folks who commit to the work, the lesser the work on any one individual. Let me explain!

About me

I'm a niche streamer. I've recently finished streaming reviews of the 151 Dosaku games we have. I recently decided to start reviewing every Shusaku game out there, chronologically. That's 471 games. I review two games, on average, every week, on my Sunday stream. Some games are shorter, of course, but with quick math you get to over four years of streams. I've done four games so far, and it has occurred to me that reviewing Shusaku games is *difficult*. Which, I realize, probably doesn't surprise anyone.

What's in it for you

This takes me to the point. I am looking for folks who are interested in studying the games together, as a group, and put together the synthesis of our work into a review. We can make this review public, and I can present some version of this review to the (admittedly small) audience on my Twitch stream.

You might be thinking "wow wow, you take all the credit for MY hard work?" Well I'm here to tell you, "nah, man." ... Okay, I'll elaborate.

I am happy to credit all the folks who contributed to the analysis for each game they did contribute to. I will be presenting the results of the work (and putting in a good chunk of my own), but I will also be trying to present the games in a way that works for my stream. This means, for instance: how long the review takes, the theme of the review, if any, which elements of the analysis I bring in for any given review. So, of course, not everything we cover in the review will necessarily be presented into the stream.

Second, the analysis of the games, itself, will be its own reward. The things you'll learn and discover through this work will stay with you much more deeply than for anyone who'll just be watching the stream.

Third, you'll get to meet and work with like-minded folks, all interested in the depth of game of Go, regardless of where in the world they are.

Fourth, you'll get to learn and put in practice, with help from my teacher, a systematic and thorough method for studying pro games, which I'll summarize next.

And fifth, making this review public means that we're doing a public service for the world of Go forever: people in the world can still refer to Invincible, but will no longer be bound to it, and everyone will be able to refer to this work forever.

What the work will look like

So, here's what the work is going to look like. We're going to make three passes for each game, one pass per week, using the core skills of the game of go. If you're not sure what those core skills are, my teacher will happily provide you with an introduction to these skills and what it means to analyze a game using each as a separate lens.

Step 1
Put game through katago with enough visits (10-20k visits per board position, probably), add relevant variations to SGF. For this step, my teacher has already volunteered
Step 2
Go through the game, focusing only on positional judgment.
Step 3
Go through the game, focusing only on direction of play.
Step 4
Go through the game, focusing only on reading.
Step 5
Put it all together!

We will cycle through the games, bringing in more into rotation. Every week, two games move to the next step. Which is to say, every week, two new games go through step 1, and when we're going full steam, every week, the reviews for two games are finished.

NOTE -- we're likely going to shift to one game per step soon, it turns out to be way more work than we expected to do even two games, so we probably won't be able to do six games per week unless there's a lot more people involved :D

Practical considerations

Since anyone in the world could join this effort, we must prepare for the work to be done asynchronously, though it is easier if groups can self-organize around a given day/time to meet and work together (when2meet is a great tool for this, it provides a 24-hour week or monthview, so we can check for group availability at any point of the day).

We will upload each game, with katago variations, to a specific spot on OGS, and everyone will be able to fork the game and provide their own reviews. My discord server will also have a dedicated spot for Shusaku study, with a voice channel for each analysis section, so that groups can gather at any time, including organically and opportunistically, as well. The synthesis step will happen on Saturdays, led by my teacher.

So, if you want to join the group, join my discord and express your intent. We will start this at the end of April, and I'll continue recruiting for this effort regularly.

How about using existing information? Have you heard about Invincible?

We won't be using any source material for this project, other than the game records. There are a few reasons for this:

  • Copyright law is complicated and this project is global, which makes things slightly more annoying
  • This project is about getting better at doing the work, not doing research, or comparing our work to others'
  • Most or all prior art didn't have katago available

Where will the project store the work?

OGS has a very convenient system showing who has created reviews for any given game. We will leverage this to connect everyone's work to each review, which will make it easier for us to find that work and include it in each synthesis step.

In practice, this means:

Each game will be uploaded to a properly named collection on OGS:

  • Games with katago-curated variations will be uploaded to a "katago" collection
  • Games with the synthesis of positional judgment will be uploaded to a "positional judgment" collection
  • Games with the synthesis of direction of play will be uploaded to a "direction of play" collection
  • Games with the synthesis of reading, finally, will be uploaded to a "final" collection

The games, once ready, will also be linked from this page.

Using Discord

The voice and text channels named after fundamental skills (positional judgment, direction of play, reading) can be used each week for the two games on each step. The synthesis voice and text channels are intended to be used on Saturdays.

Working synchronously
